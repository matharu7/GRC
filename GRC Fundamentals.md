# 📘 GRC FUNDAMENTALS – NOTES

## 1. 🔹 What is GRC?

**GRC (Governance, Risk, and Compliance)** is a structured approach used by organizations to:

* **Govern** operations effectively
* **Manage risks** proactively
* **Ensure compliance** with laws, regulations, and standards

👉 In simple terms:
**GRC = Doing the right things (Governance) + Avoiding problems (Risk) + Following rules (Compliance)**

---

## 2. 🔹 Core Components of GRC

### 🏛️ A. Governance

**Definition:**
Framework of rules, practices, and processes used to direct and control an organization.

**Key Points:**

* Aligns IT/security with business goals
* Defines roles & responsibilities
* Ensures accountability

**Examples:**

* Security policies
* Organizational structure
* Decision-making processes

---

### ⚠️ B. Risk Management

**Definition:**
Process of identifying, analyzing, and mitigating risks.

**Key Points:**

* Identify threats & vulnerabilities
* Assess impact and likelihood
* Apply controls to reduce risk

**Examples:**

* Data breach risk
* System downtime
* Insider threats

---

### 📜 C. Compliance

**Definition:**
Adherence to laws, regulations, standards, and internal policies.

**Key Points:**

* Avoid legal penalties
* Meet industry standards
* Maintain trust

**Examples:**

* Data protection laws
* Industry certifications
* Internal audits

---

## 3. 🔹 Objectives of GRC

* Improve decision-making
* Reduce business risks
* Ensure regulatory compliance
* Protect organizational assets
* Build customer trust

---

## 4. 🔹 Importance of GRC

* Prevents financial losses
* Avoids legal penalties
* Enhances security posture
* Supports business continuity
* Improves reputation

---

## 5. 🔹 Key GRC Principles

* **Accountability** – Clear ownership
* **Transparency** – Open processes
* **Integrity** – Ethical operations
* **Consistency** – Standardized processes

---

## 6. 🔹 GRC in Cybersecurity

GRC plays a major role in:

* Information security management
* Data protection
* Regulatory compliance

**Example:**

* Creating password policies
* Managing access control
* Ensuring audit readiness

---

## 7. 🔹 GRC Lifecycle (Basic Flow)

1. Identify requirements (laws, risks)
2. Define policies & controls
3. Implement controls
4. Monitor & review
5. Improve continuously

---

## 8. 🔹 Roles in GRC

* **GRC Analyst** – Handles risk & compliance tasks
* **Security Officer** – Ensures security controls
* **Auditor** – Reviews compliance
* **Management** – Makes decisions

---

## 9. 🔹 Real-World Example

**Scenario:**
A company stores customer data.

* **Governance:** Creates data protection policy
* **Risk:** Identifies risk of data breach
* **Compliance:** Follows data protection laws

---

* Risk
