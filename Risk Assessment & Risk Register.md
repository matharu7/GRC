# 📘 3. RISK ASSESSMENT & RISK REGISTER – NOTES

---

## 1. 🔹 What is Risk?

**Definition:**
A **risk** is the possibility of a threat exploiting a vulnerability, causing harm to an organization.

👉 Simple formula:
**Risk = Threat × Vulnerability × Impact**

---

## 2. 🔹 What is Risk Assessment?

**Definition:**
The process of:

* Identifying risks
* Analyzing risks
* Evaluating risks

👉 Goal: **Understand and prioritize risks**

---

## 3. 🔹 Objectives of Risk Assessment

* Identify potential threats
* Evaluate impact on business
* Prioritize risks
* Apply appropriate controls
* Support decision-making

---

## 4. 🔹 Key Components of Risk

* **Asset** – What needs protection (data, systems)
* **Threat** – Potential danger (hackers, malware)
* **Vulnerability** – Weakness (no firewall, weak password)
* **Impact** – Damage caused (financial, reputation)
* **Likelihood** – Chance of occurrence

---

## 5. 🔹 Risk Assessment Process

### Step 1: Identify Assets

* Data, systems, people, processes

### Step 2: Identify Threats

* Cyber attacks
* Insider threats
* Natural disasters

### Step 3: Identify Vulnerabilities

* Weak passwords
* Outdated software
* Lack of controls

### Step 4: Analyze Risk

* Likelihood (Low/Medium/High)
* Impact (Low/Medium/High)

### Step 5: Evaluate Risk

* Prioritize based on severity

---

## 6. 🔹 Risk Levels (Example)

| Likelihood | Impact | Risk Level |
| ---------- | ------ | ---------- |
| High       | High   | Critical   |
| Medium     | High   | High       |
| Low        | Medium | Medium     |
| Low        | Low    | Low        |

---

## 7. 🔹 Risk Treatment Options

* **Avoid** – Stop the activity
* **Mitigate** – Reduce risk (apply controls)
* **Transfer** – Insurance / third party
* **Accept** – Accept low risk

---

## 8. 🔹 What is a Risk Register?

**Definition:**
A **Risk Register** is a central document used to:

* Record identified risks
* Track risk status
* Manage mitigation actions

👉 It is a **key GRC document**

---

## 9. 🔹 Risk Register Format (Sample)

| Risk ID | Asset    | Threat      | Vulnerability | Likelihood | Impact | Risk Level | Owner   | Mitigation                     |
| ------- | -------- | ----------- | ------------- | ---------- | ------ | ---------- | ------- | ------------------------------ |
| R1      | Database | Data Breach | Weak Password | High       | High   | Critical   | IT Head | Enforce strong password policy |
| R2      | Server   | Malware     | No Antivirus  | Medium     | High   | High       | Admin   | Install antivirus              |

---

## 10. 🔹 Key Fields in Risk Register

* Risk ID
* Description
* Asset
* Threat & Vulnerability
* Likelihood & Impact
* Risk Rating
* Risk Owner
* Mitigation Plan
* Status

---

## 11. 🔹 Qualitative vs Quantitative Risk

### 📊 Qualitative

* Based on judgment
* Uses High/Medium/Low

### 🔢 Quantitative

* Uses numbers (financial impact)
* More accurate but complex

---

## 12. 🔹 Tools Used

* Excel (most common)
* GRC tools (e.g., ServiceNow, Archer)
* Risk management software

---

## 13. 🔹 Real-World Example

**Scenario:**
Company website is outdated.

* **Asset:** Website
* **Threat:** Hacker attack
* **Vulnerability:** Unpatched software
* **Impact:** Data loss
* **Risk Level:** High
* **Mitigation:** Update system regularly

---

## 14. 🔹 Common Mistakes

* Not updating risk register
* Ignoring low risks (they can grow)
* No clear risk owner
* Poor documentation
* No follow-up on mitigation
