

# 📘 5. SECURITY AUDITS (INTERNAL / EXTERNAL) – NOTES

---

## 1. 🔹 What is a Security Audit?

**Definition:**
A **security audit** is a systematic evaluation of:

* Security controls
* Policies
* Processes

👉 To ensure they are:

* Effective
* Properly implemented
* Compliant with standards

---

## 2. 🔹 Objectives of Security Audits

* Verify compliance with regulations
* Identify security gaps
* Improve controls
* Ensure policies are followed
* Prepare for certifications

---

## 3. 🔹 Types of Security Audits

### 🏢 A. Internal Audit

**Definition:**
Conducted by internal team or organization staff.

**Key Points:**

* Done regularly (quarterly/yearly)
* Helps in continuous improvement
* Less formal than external audits

**Purpose:**

* Self-assessment
* Identify gaps before external audit

---

### 🌍 B. External Audit

**Definition:**
Conducted by independent third-party auditors.

**Key Points:**

* Required for certifications & compliance
* More formal and strict
* Provides unbiased evaluation

**Purpose:**

* Certification (e.g., ISO)
* Regulatory compliance

---

## 4. 🔹 Audit vs Compliance

### 📊 Audit

* Process of checking
* Evaluates controls

### 📜 Compliance

* State of following rules

👉 **Audit checks → Compliance confirms**

---

## 5. 🔹 Audit Process (Step-by-Step)

### Step 1: Planning

* Define scope
* Identify standards (ISO, etc.)

### Step 2: Preparation

* Collect documents
* Review policies

### Step 3: Fieldwork

* Interviews
* System checks
* Evidence collection

### Step 4: Analysis

* Identify gaps
* Evaluate risks

### Step 5: Reporting

* Audit report
* Findings & recommendations

### Step 6: Follow-up

* Fix issues
* Verify improvements

---

## 6. 🔹 Types of Audit Findings

* **Compliant** – Everything is correct
* **Non-Conformity (NC)** – Requirement not met
* **Observation** – Minor issue
* **Opportunity for Improvement (OFI)**

---

## 7. 🔹 Evidence in Audits

Auditors require proof such as:

* Policies & procedures
* System logs
* Access control records
* Training records
* Screenshots

👉 Rule: **“If it’s not documented, it doesn’t exist.”**

---

## 8. 🔹 Common Areas Audited

* Access control
* Password policies
* Data protection
* Incident response
* Backup & recovery
* Vendor management

---

## 9. 🔹 Compliance in GRC

**Definition:**
Ensuring organization follows:

* Laws
* Regulations
* Standards

---

## 10. 🔹 Examples of Compliance Requirements

* Data protection laws
* Industry standards
* Internal policies

---

## 11. 🔹 Benefits of Audits & Compliance

* Avoid penalties
* Improve security posture
* Build customer trust
* Achieve certifications
* Detect weaknesses early

---

## 12. 🔹 Common Audit Mistakes

* Poor documentation
* Untrained employees
* No evidence available
* Ignoring previous findings
* Last-minute preparation

---

## 13. 🔹 Real-World Example

**Scenario:**
Company claims strong password policy.

* **Audit Check:** Review system settings
* **Finding:** Weak passwords allowed
* **Result:** Non-compliance
* **Action:** Enforce policy

---

## 14. 🔹 Internal vs External Audit (Quick Comparison)

| Feature      | Internal Audit | External Audit |
| ------------ | -------------- | -------------- |
| Who performs | Internal team  | Third-party    |
| Purpose      | Improvement    | Certification  |
| Frequency    | Regular        | Periodic       |
| Cost         | Low            | High           |
| Objectivity  | Moderate       | High           |
