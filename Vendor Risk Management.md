
# 📘 6. VENDOR RISK MANAGEMENT (VRM) – NOTES

---

## 1. 🔹 What is Vendor Risk Management?

**Definition:**
**Vendor Risk Management (VRM)** is the process of:

* Identifying
* Assessing
* Monitoring

👉 Risks associated with **third-party vendors / suppliers**

---

## 2. 🔹 Who is a Vendor?

A **vendor (third party)** is any external entity that:

* Provides services
* Handles company data
* Supports operations

**Examples:**

* Cloud providers
* Payment processors
* IT service companies
* Outsourcing partners

---

## 3. 🔹 Why Vendor Risk Management is Important

* Vendors may access sensitive data
* Weak vendor security = your risk
* Required for compliance
* Prevents data breaches
* Ensures business continuity

👉 **Key Idea:**
**“Your security is only as strong as your weakest vendor.”**

---

## 4. 🔹 Types of Vendor Risks

### 🔐 1. Security Risk

* Data breaches
* Weak controls

### ⚖️ 2. Compliance Risk

* Vendor not following regulations

### 🔄 3. Operational Risk

* Service downtime
* Poor performance

### 💰 4. Financial Risk

* Vendor bankruptcy

### 🌐 5. Reputational Risk

* Vendor actions affect your brand

---

## 5. 🔹 Vendor Risk Management Lifecycle

### Step 1: Vendor Identification

* List all vendors
* Categorize (critical / non-critical)

---

### Step 2: Risk Assessment

* Evaluate vendor security
* Check data access level
* Use questionnaires

---

### Step 3: Due Diligence

* Review certifications
* Check policies
* Evaluate past incidents

---

### Step 4: Contract & Agreement

* Define security requirements
* Include SLA (Service Level Agreement)
* Add data protection clauses

---

### Step 5: Continuous Monitoring

* Regular reviews
* Security checks
* Performance tracking

---

### Step 6: Offboarding

* Remove access
* Ensure data return/deletion

---

## 6. 🔹 Vendor Risk Assessment Criteria

* Data sensitivity
* Access level
* Security controls
* Compliance status
* Business criticality

---

## 7. 🔹 Vendor Risk Levels

| Vendor Type       | Risk Level | Example         |
| ----------------- | ---------- | --------------- |
| Critical Vendor   | High       | Cloud provider  |
| Important Vendor  | Medium     | IT support      |
| Low Impact Vendor | Low        | Office supplies |

---

## 8. 🔹 Common Documents in VRM

* Vendor inventory list
* Risk assessment questionnaire
* NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement)
* SLA (Service Level Agreement)
* Security agreement

---

## 9. 🔹 Vendor Security Checklist (Basic)

* Do they have security policies?
* Are they compliant with standards?
* Do they use encryption?
* Do they have incident response?
* Do they perform audits?

---

## 10. 🔹 Real-World Example

**Scenario:**
Company uses a cloud storage vendor.

* **Risk:** Data breach
* **Check:** Vendor security controls
* **Action:** Sign data protection agreement
* **Monitor:** Regular audits

---

## 11. 🔹 Common Mistakes

* Not assessing vendors properly
* No continuous monitoring
* Missing contract clauses
* Ignoring small vendors
* No offboarding process

---

## 12. 🔹 Tools Used

* Excel (vendor tracking)
* GRC tools (ServiceNow, Archer)
* Vendor risk platforms

---

## 13. 🔹 Benefits of VRM

* Reduces third-party risks
* Ensures compliance
* Protects sensitive data
* Improves vendor accountability
