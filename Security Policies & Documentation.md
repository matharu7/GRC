# 📘 2. SECURITY POLICIES & DOCUMENTATION – NOTES

## 1. 🔹 What is a Security Policy?

**Definition:**
A **security policy** is a formal document that defines:

* Rules
* Guidelines
* Procedures

👉 It tells employees **what is allowed and what is not** regarding security.

---

## 2. 🔹 Why Security Policies are Important

* Protect sensitive data
* Reduce security risks
* Ensure compliance with laws
* Provide clear instructions to employees
* Support audits and investigations

---

## 3. 🔹 Types of Security Documents (Hierarchy)

### 🏛️ 1. Policies (High-Level)

* Broad rules and intentions
* Approved by top management

**Example:**

* Information Security Policy
* Data Protection Policy

---

### 📋 2. Standards (Mandatory Rules)

* Specific technical requirements
* Must be followed strictly

**Example:**

* Password must be at least 12 characters
* Use AES-256 encryption

---

### 🧭 3. Procedures (Step-by-Step)

* How to perform tasks

**Example:**

* How to create a user account
* How to respond to incidents

---

### 📘 4. Guidelines (Recommended Practices)

* Not mandatory but helpful

**Example:**

* Best practices for secure coding

---

## 4. 🔹 Key Security Policies (Common in Organizations)

* Information Security Policy
* Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)
* Password Policy
* Access Control Policy
* Data Classification Policy
* Incident Response Policy
* Backup & Recovery Policy

---

## 5. 🔹 Components of a Good Security Policy

* **Purpose** – Why the policy exists
* **Scope** – Who and what it applies to
* **Definitions** – Key terms
* **Policy Statements** – Rules
* **Roles & Responsibilities**
* **Enforcement** – Consequences of violations
* **Review Cycle** – Updates

---

## 6. 🔹 Documentation in GRC

Documentation is critical for:

* Audit evidence
* Compliance proof
* Process consistency
* Knowledge sharing

---

## 7. 🔹 Policy Lifecycle

1. Draft policy
2. Review & approve
3. Communicate to employees
4. Implement
5. Monitor compliance
6. Update regularly

---

## 8. 🔹 Best Practices

* Keep policies **clear and simple**
* Avoid too much technical jargon
* Update regularly (at least annually)
* Ensure employee awareness
* Align with business goals

---

## 9. 🔹 Common Mistakes

* Policies are too complex
* Not updated regularly
* Employees unaware of policies
* No enforcement
* Copy-paste from other companies

---

## 10. 🔹 Real-World Example

**Scenario:**
An employee uses personal email for work files.

* **Policy:** Acceptable Use Policy
* **Rule:** No sharing of company data externally
* **Risk:** Data leakage
* **Control:** Block external sharing

---
