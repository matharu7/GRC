#  GRC Glossary

## 📌 Overview

This glossary provides definitions of key terms used in **Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC)**.
It helps learners quickly understand important concepts used across all modules.

---

## 🔤 A

**Asset**
Any resource of value to an organization (e.g., data, systems, people, hardware).

**Audit**
A systematic review of systems, policies, and controls to ensure compliance and effectiveness.

---

## 🔤 C

**Compliance**
Adherence to laws, regulations, standards, and internal policies.

**Control**
A safeguard or measure implemented to reduce risk.

**Confidentiality**
Ensuring that information is accessible only to authorized users.

---

## 🔤 D

**Data Breach**
Unauthorized access, disclosure, or loss of sensitive information.

---

## 🔤 G

**Governance**
Framework of rules and processes used to direct and control an organization.

---

## 🔤 I

**Impact**
The potential damage or loss caused by a risk or incident.

**Incident**
An event that compromises security, policies, or operations.

**Integrity**
Ensuring accuracy and reliability of data.

---

## 🔤 L

**Likelihood**
The probability that a risk will occur.

---

## 🔤 M

**Mitigation**
Actions taken to reduce the severity or likelihood of a risk.

---

## 🔤 P

**Policy**
A high-level document defining rules and expectations.

**Procedure**
Step-by-step instructions to perform a task.

---

## 🔤 R

**Risk**
The possibility of a threat exploiting a vulnerability causing harm.

**Risk Assessment**
The process of identifying, analyzing, and evaluating risks.

**Risk Register**
A document used to record and track identified risks.

---

## 🔤 S

**Security Incident**
An event that compromises confidentiality, integrity, or availability.

**Standard**
Mandatory requirements that must be followed.

---

## 🔤 T

**Threat**
Any potential cause of harm (e.g., hacker, malware).

---

## 🔤 V

**Vulnerability**
A weakness that can be exploited by a threat.

**Vendor**
A third-party organization providing services or products.

---
